ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Using Binding Arbitration to Resolve Family Law Disputes

ARBITRATION

Arbitration is a form of alternative dispute resolution that allows parties to seek a binding decision on issues in dispute in their family law matter. The benefit of arbitration is that it is a private dispute resolution system, meaning there is no public record of the materials filed.

Arbitration requires the parties to retain the services of an arbitrator to decide their case. As this is a private arrangement, the parties can agree on how they wish the arbitration to be conducted and are able to effectively schedule their arbitration based on their schedule. Moreover, because the arbitration is private, the parties are able to select an agreed upon professional to act in deciding their case.

Arbitration is far quicker than going to court, ensure discretion in discussing financial issues, and can lead to an equitable decision relating to family law issues.

Arbitrations are binding and have a similar effect as a Court order. Arbitrations are not limited to making final decision in matters. Arbitrators can also assist parties in deciding ‘temporary issues’, for example financial disclosure, ongoing parenting issues, temporary child support.
