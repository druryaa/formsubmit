Decision-Making Following Spearation

DECISION-MAKING RESPONSIBILITY

Decision-making responsibility concerns the right to make decision for a child. This can include a child's doctor, acceptance of medical treatment, a choice of school, and other major issues impacting a child's life. Typically, decision-making responsibility does not include day-to-day decisions.

A central component to a constructive co-parenting arrangement is the ability to communicate with the other parent. It is imperative that parents attempt to resolve issues concerning their children constructively. In the event that parents can work through decisions for their children, it is likely that a joint decision-making regime will be put in place for the benefit of the children. Parents who have a joint decision-making regime, will make decisions jointly for the benefit of their children. In the event of a disagreement, they will need to work through those disputes and come to a conclusion in a child-focus manner.

In the event that the parties cannot agree on issues concerning the children, it may be the case that a sole decision-making regime will need to be implemented. When determining which parent ought to have the ability to make decisions for the children, the following factors are usually considered:

• Which parent has typically made decisions for the children?

• Which parent is more likely to foster a relationship with the other?

• Which parent is primarily responsible for the children?

• Which parent, if either, seems to be the source of the conflict or inability to communicate?

A parent who has sole decision-making responsibility for their children, are able to make major decisions in the face of an objection from the other parent. However, this does not permit them to make decisions to the exclusion of the other parent. In fact, a parent with sole decision-making responsibility has an obligation to consult with the other parent prior to making a decision for the benefit one or more children. The duty to consult with the other parent ensures that both parents have input into their children's lives.
