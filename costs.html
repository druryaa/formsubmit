COSTS

Protection from Litigation

WHAT ARE COSTS AND WHEN ARE THEY PAID?

Costs are typically awarded to the successful party, following a decision from the Court on a Motion, Trial, or other substantial step in a case.

Costs are entirely discretionary and up to the Judge hearing the matter. The reasonableness of the litigants is usually front-and-center on any award for costs.

In addition, it is important to put your best foot forward in trying to resolve the issue in dispute. Any efforts to resolve a hearing, may be put to the Judge, when deciding costs. In fact, the Rules of Court specifically encourage exchanging offers to settle, in order to cut down on the number of disputes that require Judicial intervention. It is vital that parties make reasonable offers to settle, when possible.

Costs are a very serious consideration for the Court. They can be used to curtail tactical litigation, and prevent vexatious litigants from engaging in poor behaviour.

SECURITY FOR COSTS

The issuance of an order for costs has serious consequences. A person who refuses to pay costs can have their ability to participate in the proceeding seriously modified or even revoked.

Our firm successfuly represented a client in requiring a litigant to pay outstanding costs, and also pay "up-front" security for future costs, based upon his conduct in the litigation, in the case of Hevey v Hevey . Non-payment of costs, can have serious consequences for the parties involved in family law litigation.
