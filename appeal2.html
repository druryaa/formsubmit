Appealing an Arbitrators Award

APPEALING ARBITRATORS AWARD

The Arbitration Act provides that a party may appeal an arbitral award on a question of law or mixed fact and law, if the arbitration agreement so provides. When appealing arbitrators’ awards, they are appealed to the Ontario Superior Court of Justice in Ontario and the Court of Kings Bench in Alberta. When considering an appeal of an arbitrator award, Appellate courts have broad discretion when considering matters related to the application of law.

The starting point for considering an appeal is to review the Arbitration Agreement, which is the contract signed between the parties and the arbitrator. The Arbitration Agreement is the source of the arbitrator’s ability to adjudicate the matter and will provide for the types of appeals that are permitted.

Appealing arbitrators awards typically requires the appellant to demonstrate that the arbitrator made an error in their application of the law. This could include an arbitrator failing to consider certain factors in a legal test, or an incorrect application of a legal principle. When appealing an arbitration award on the basis of a failure to apply the law correctly, the standard of review is called “correctness”. This means that the moving party on the appeal has to persuade an appellate authority that the arbitration award did not correctly apply the law.

If the appeal of the arbitration award is attempting to address the application of law to a set of facts, it is a question of mixed fact and law. A question of mixed fact and law is treated differently than a straight question of law and requires a higher standard of review then simply “correctness”. The appellant will typically need to persuade an appellate authority of palpable and overriding error.

Appealing an arbitration award is not too dissimilar to appealing a final order of a lower Court. In both types of appeals, there are strict statute-imposed timetables for when to commence the appeal. It is essential that you act quickly if you are dissatisfied with an arbitration award.
